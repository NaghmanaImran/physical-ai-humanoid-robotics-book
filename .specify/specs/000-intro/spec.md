# 000-intro: Introduction to Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics

## Title
Introduction to Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics

## Focus
This chapter introduces the fundamental concepts of Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics, setting the foundation for the entire course. It covers the history, current state, and future directions of embodied intelligence.

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this chapter, students will be able to:
- Define Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics
- Understand the historical context and evolution of humanoid robots
- Identify key challenges and opportunities in the field
- Recognize the interdisciplinary nature of the field (mechanics, electronics, AI)
- Explain the importance of embodied intelligence in robotics

## Required Subsections

### 1.1 What is Physical AI?
Definition and importance of intelligence that exists in physical form, interacting with the real world through sensors and actuators.

### 1.2 History and Evolution of Humanoid Robotics
Chronological overview of humanoid robotics development from early concepts to modern implementations.

### 1.3 Interdisciplinary Nature of Humanoid Robotics
Exploration of how mechanical engineering, electronics, computer science, and AI converge in humanoid robotics.

### 1.4 Current State and Applications
Overview of existing humanoid robots and their applications in various fields (industrial, service, research).

### 1.5 Future Directions and Challenges
Discussion of emerging trends, technologies, and challenges that need to be addressed.

### 1.6 Course Overview
Introduction to the modules covered in this textbook and how they connect to build a complete humanoid robot system.

## Hands-on Labs
- Lab 1: Explore existing humanoid robots through videos and documentation
- Lab 2: Basic simulation environment setup (Gazebo, Unity, or Isaac Sim)
- Lab 3: Introduction to robot modeling and kinematics

## Key Takeaways
- Physical AI requires integration of perception, reasoning, and action in real-world environments
- Humanoid robots represent a complex integration of multiple technologies
- The field is rapidly evolving with new breakthroughs regularly
- Success requires knowledge across multiple disciplines
